Suppose that we wish to study wallpaper patterns in the plane or crystals in three dimensions. Wallpaper patterns are simply repeating patterns in the plane (Figure 12.2.5). The analogs of wallpaper patterns in \({\mathbb R}^3\) are crystals, which we can think of as repeating patterns of molecules in three dimensions (Figure 12.2.6). The mathematical equivalent of a wallpaper or crystal pattern is called a lattice.
