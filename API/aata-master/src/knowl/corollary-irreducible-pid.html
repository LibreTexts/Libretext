Corollary 18.2.6.

Let \(D\) be a PID. If \(p\) is irreducible, then \(p\) is prime.

Proof.

Let \(p\) be irreducible and suppose that \(p \mid ab\text{.}\) Then \(\langle ab \rangle \subset \langle p \rangle\text{.}\) By CorollaryÂ 16.4.6, since \(\langle p \rangle\) is a maximal ideal, \(\langle p \rangle\) must also be a prime ideal. Thus, either \(a \in \langle p \rangle\) or \(b \in \langle p \rangle\text{.}\) Hence, either \(p \mid a \) or \(p \mid b\text{.}\)
