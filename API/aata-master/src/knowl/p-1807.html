The second paragraph of this chapter informally describes a homomorphism from \(S_n\) to \({\mathbb Z}_2\text{,}\) where the even permutations all map to one of the elements and the odd permutations all map to the other element. Replace \(S_n\) by \(S_6\) and replace \({\mathbb Z}_2\) by the permutation version of the cyclic subgroup of order \(2\text{,}\) and construct a nontrivial homomorphism between these two groups. Evaluate your homomorphism with enough even and odd permutations to be convinced that it is correct. Then construct the kernel and verify that it is the group you expect.
