An extension field \(E\) of a field \(F\) is an extension by radicals if there exists a chain of subfields

\begin{equation*} F = F_0 \subset F_1 \subset F_2 \subset \cdots \subset F_r = E \end{equation*}

such for \(i = 1, 2, \ldots, r\text{,}\) we have \(F_i = F_{i - 1}(\alpha_i)\) and \(\alpha_i^{n_i} \in F_{i-1}\) for some positive integer \(n_i\text{.}\) A polynomial \(f(x)\) is solvable by radicals over \(F\) if the splitting field \(K\) of \(f(x)\) over \(F\) is contained in an extension of \(F\) by radicals. Our goal is to arrive at criteria that will tell us whether or not a polynomial \(f(x)\) is solvable by radicals by examining the Galois group \(f(x)\text{.}\)
