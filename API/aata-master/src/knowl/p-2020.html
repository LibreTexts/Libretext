It does not take any sophisticated tools to understand groups of order \(2p\text{,}\) where \(p\) is an odd prime. There are two possibilities — a cyclic group of order \(2p\) and the dihedral group of order \(2p\) that is the set of symmetries of a regular \(p\)-gon. The proof requires some close, tight reasoning, but the required theorems are generally just concern orders of elements, Lagrange's Theorem and cosets. See Exercise 9.3.55. This takes care of orders \(n=6,\,10,\,14\text{.}\)
