The Sage permutation group method .sylow_subgroup(p)will return a single Sylow \(p\)-subgroup. If the prime is not a proper divisor of the group order it returns a subgroup of order \(p^0\text{,}\) in other words, a trivial subgroup. So be careful about how you construct your primes. Sometimes, you may only want one such Sylow subgroup, since any two Sylow \(p\)-subgroups are conjugate, and hence isomorphic (TheoremÂ 15.1.7). This also means we can create other Sylow \(p\)-subgroups by conjugating the one we have. The permutation group method .conjugate(g)will conjugate the group by g.
