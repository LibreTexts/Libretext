Corollary 6.2.5.

Let \(H\) and \(K\) be subgroups of a finite group \(G\) such that \(G \supset H \supset K\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} [G:K] = [G:H][H:K]\text{.} \end{equation*}
Proof.

Observe that

\begin{equation*} [G:K] = \frac{|G|}{|K|} = \frac{|G|}{|H|} \cdot \frac{|H|}{|K|} = [G:H][H:K]\text{.} \end{equation*}
