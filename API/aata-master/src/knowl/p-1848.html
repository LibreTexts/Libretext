Let us examine the orthogonal group on \({\mathbb R}^2\) a bit more closely. An element \(T \in O(2)\) is determined by its action on \({\mathbf e}_1 = (1, 0)^\transpose\) and \({\mathbf e}_2 = (0, 1)^\transpose\text{.}\) If \(T({\mathbf e}_1) = (a,b)^\transpose\text{,}\) then \(a^2 + b^2 = 1\) and \(T({\mathbf e}_2) = (-b, a)^\transpose\text{.}\) Hence, \(T\) can be represented by

\begin{equation*} A = \begin{pmatrix} a & -b \\ b & a \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} \cos \theta & - \sin \theta \\ \sin \theta & \cos \theta \end{pmatrix}\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(0 \leq \theta \lt 2 \pi\text{.}\) A matrix \(T\) in \(O(2)\) either reflects or rotates a vector in \({\mathbb R}^2\) (FigureÂ 12.1.9). A reflection about the horizontal axis is given by the matrix

\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}\text{,} \end{equation*}

whereas a rotation by an angle \(\theta\) in a counterclockwise direction must come from a matrix of the form

\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} \cos \theta & \sin \theta \\ \sin \theta & -\cos \theta \end{pmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

A reflection about a line \(\ell\) is simply a reflection about the horizontal axis followed by a rotation. If \(\det A =-1\text{,}\) then \(A\) gives a reflection.
