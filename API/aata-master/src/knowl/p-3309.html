Let \(F\) be a subfield of \({\mathbb R}\text{.}\)

  1. If a line contains two points in \(F\text{,}\) then it has the equation \(a x + by + c = 0\text{,}\) where \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(c\) are in \(F\text{.}\)

  2. If a circle has a center at a point with coordinates in \(F\) and a radius that is also in \(F\text{,}\) then it has the equation \(x^2 + y^2 + d x + e y + f = 0\text{,}\) where \(d\text{,}\) \(e\text{,}\) and \(f\) are in \(F\text{.}\)
