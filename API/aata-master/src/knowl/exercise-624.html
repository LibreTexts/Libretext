Exercise 4.

Define the polynomials \(p = x^3 + 2x^2 + 2x + 4\) and \(q = x^4 + 2x^2\) as polynomials with coefficients from the integers. Compute gcd(p, q)and verify that the result divides both pand q(just form a fraction in Sage and see that it simplifies cleanly, or use the .quo_rem()method).

Proposition 17.2.5 says there are polynomials \(r(x)\) and \(s(x)\) such that the greatest common divisor equals \(r(x)p(x)+s(x)q(x)\text{,}\) if the coefficients come from a field. Since here we have two polynomials over the integers, investigate the results returned by Sage for the extended gcd, xgcd(p, q). In particular, show that the first result of the returned triple is a multiple of the gcd. Then verify the “linear combination” property of the result.
