Let \(E\) be any other field of order \(p^n\text{.}\) To show that \(E\) is isomorphic to \(F\text{,}\) we must show that every element in \(E\) is a root of \(f(x)\text{.}\) Certainly 0 is a root of \(f(x)\text{.}\) Let \(\alpha\) be a nonzero element of \(E\text{.}\) The order of the multiplicative group of nonzero elements of \(E\) is \(p^n-1\text{;}\) hence, \(\alpha^{p^n-1} =1\) or \(\alpha^{p^n} -\alpha = 0\text{.}\) Since \(E\) contains \(p^n\) elements, \(E\) must be a splitting field of \(f(x)\text{;}\) however, by CorollaryÂ 21.2.6, the splitting field of any polynomial is unique up to isomorphism.
