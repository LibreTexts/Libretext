Theorem 6.3.2. Euler's Theorem.

Let \(a\) and \(n\) be integers such that \(n \gt 0\) and \(\gcd(a, n) = 1\text{.}\) Then \(a^{\phi(n)} \equiv 1 \pmod{n}\text{.}\)

By TheoremÂ 6.3.1 the order of \(U(n)\) is \(\phi(n)\text{.}\) Consequently, \(a^{\phi(n)} = 1\) for all \(a \in U(n)\text{;}\) or \(a^{\phi(n)} - 1\) is divisible by \(n\text{.}\) Therefore, \(a^{\phi(n)} \equiv 1 \pmod{n}\text{.}\)
